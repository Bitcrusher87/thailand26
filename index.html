Thailand Essentials

💴
Currency

The currency is the Thai Baht (THB). 1 EUR ≈ 38–40 THB. Use ATMs throughout Thailand — they're everywhere. Bangkok ATMs charge ±220 THB fee per withdrawal so take out larger amounts (5,000–10,000 THB at a time). Always carry some cash as smaller restaurants, markets and temples are cash only. Weather

Late May–June is the beginning of Thailand's rainy season (green season). Expect heat and humidity throughout — Bangkok 33–36°C, Chiang Mai 30–34°C, Koh Tao 30–33°C. Afternoon showers are common but usually short. Bring a light rain layer or poncho. Koh Tao has excellent diving visibility in early June. Chiang Mai's mountain areas (Doi Inthanon) can be 15–18°C — bring a layer. More about Thailand climate →

🛕
Temple Etiquette

Thailand has over 40,000 Buddhist temples. When visiting:

  • Cover shoulders and knees at all times
  • Remove shoes before entering any chapel
  • Never point feet toward Buddha images
  • Women must not touch or hand anything directly to monks
  • Keep voices low — these are active places of worship
  • No flash photography inside chapels
More about Buddhism in Thailand →
🚕
Getting Around

Grab is the essential app — it's the Uber of Southeast Asia. Always use it over street taxis to avoid price negotiations. BTS Skytrain and MRT Metro cover Bangkok efficiently. Tuk-tuks are for short trips only — always agree a price first. On Koh Tao, scooter rental is ±200–250 THB/day. More about Grab →

🍜
Food & Eating

Thai food is one of the world's great cuisines — fresh herbs, complex spice pastes and bright flavours. Street food is safe and excellent. Look for busy stalls with high turnover. Key dishes to try: Pad Thai, Khao Soi (Chiang Mai), Tom Yum, Som Tam, Massaman curry, Pad Kra Pao. Vegetarian options are widely available — say "jay" for vegan. More about Thai cuisine →

📱
Connectivity

Thai SIM sorted ✓ — activate immediately on arrival at Suvarnabhumi. Coverage is excellent in Bangkok and Chiang Mai. Koh Tao has patchy 4G in some bays. Download this guide, Google Maps offline areas, and any other essentials before leaving the mainland. WhatsApp and LINE are the main messaging apps used locally.

🩺
Health & Safety

Thailand is very safe for tourists. Drink bottled or filtered water only — never tap water. Apply mosquito repellent especially at dawn and dusk. Use reef-safe sunscreen on Koh Tao — standard sunscreen damages coral and is restricted in marine parks. Thailand has excellent private hospitals in all major cities. Travel insurance is essential. Healthcare in Thailand →

🙏
Culture & Customs

Thailand is known as the Land of Smiles — Thais are warm, patient and non-confrontational. A few key customs: never touch anyone on the head (the most sacred part of the body). Remove shoes at temple and home entrances. The Royal Family is deeply revered — never make negative comments. Tipping is appreciated but not mandatory: 20–50 THB at restaurants, round up taxi fares.
